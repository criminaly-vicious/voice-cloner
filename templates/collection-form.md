# Collection form

Send this form before beginning. The final skill is limited by the quality of the samples; no analysis method can compensate for poor source material.

## If the voice belongs to a person

Provide at least five texts you wrote carefully and had time to revise. For each one, state:

- Who the text was written for, because voice changes with the audience.
- Its purpose, such as persuading, informing, requesting, thanking, or documenting.
- Its perceived formality on a simple scale from very informal to very formal.
- Whether someone else or an AI edited it. Disclose this so the sample can receive less weight or be excluded.

Provide at least three speech samples, such as audio transcripts, dictated messages, or meeting excerpts. Label each as speech. Do not remove hesitations, repetitions, or unfinished sentences because those features are part of the evidence.

More contextual variety produces a better skill. Five emails to the same manager produce a skill that only knows how to write emails to that manager.

## If the voice belongs to a brand

Provide published text from at least three channels, such as the website, campaign email, social media, sales proposals, and documentation. Label every channel because the analysis depends on that distinction.

Provide any existing brand material, even when outdated, such as a manifesto, mission statement, tone guide, product glossary, or naming guide. It will not be accepted as truth; it will be compared with actual published writing.

Provide examples the brand considers off-tone, including internally rejected texts. Counterexamples are efficient evidence.

State who currently writes for the brand. A brand with five authors has five voices, and the analysis must find their intersection rather than their average.

## Context questions

Answer each in one or two sentences:

- Who is the typical reader, and what do they already know before reading?
- What reaction should the text create? Informing, persuading, and moving an audience require different tones.
- Is there any word, expression, or construction you dislike seeing associated with you or the brand?
- Is there a writer whose style you admire? This helps separate the current voice from the desired voice.
